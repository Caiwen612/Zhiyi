    Eye Palette • 10g

    RM218.00

    DESCRIPTION

    Function: Colour
    Finish: Matte, Shimmer, Metallic, Natural
    Formulation: Pressed Powder

    What it is:
    A palette that is your secret weapon to creating an array of pro eye looks, from the most natural to the most defined.

    What it does:
    Inspired by the energy backstage at the runway shows and makeup artist essentials, this eye palette includes shades that suit all skin tones in buildable matte and iridescent textures for perfect eyes. The palette is available in two universal harmonies: rosy hues with cool undertones or brown neutrals with warm undertones. The creamy primer provides long-lasting hold, but more importantly, it intensifies the effect of the colour that will be applied over it. The result is long-wearing yet richly pigmented, buildable colours with a second-skin finish.

    What it includes:
    - 1 eye primer
    - 8 powders: 3 shimmer shades, 3 ultra-pigmented matte shades, 1 highlighting hue, 1 sculpting shadow

LEARN MORE - How To Use Dior Backstage Eye Palette

MORE INFORMATION ABOUT DIOR BACKSTAGE EYE PALETTE

- Apply the primer to the entire eyelid.

- Add colour to the lid with a light shade on the brow arch.

- Intensify the eyes by applying a more intense shade along the lashline and to the outside corner of the eye.

- Deepen the eyes by applying a darker shade under the eye, then blend.

- For a natural look: Apply a more intense shade to the entire mobile eyelid to open up the eyes, then add a darker shade along the lashline to add definition. Highlight by applying a lighter shade under the eyebrow arch and to the internal corner to open up the eyes.

- For a sophisticated look: Apply a more intense shade to the entire mobile eyelid to open up the eyes, then add a darker shade along the lashline to add definition. Blend outwards on the entire eyelid. Highlight by applying a lighter shade under the eyebrow arch and at the inner corner of the eye to create an even more intense contrast that structures the eyes.
